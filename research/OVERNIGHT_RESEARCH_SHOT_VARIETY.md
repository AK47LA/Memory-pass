# 📊 RESEARCH FINDINGS

## Ideal Shot Count for 60-90 Second Videos

**Best Practice:**
- **20-30 shots** for 60-90s (not 36)
- Average **2-4 seconds per shot**
- Fast action: 1-2s
- Emotional beats: 3-5s
- Lip sync chorus: 3-4s

**Brush It Up current:**
- 36 shots / 85s = 2.4s average ✅ (acceptable)
- BUT: Could simplify to 24-28 shots for easier production

## Dynamic Shot Variety Techniques

### Camera Angles to Mix:
1. **Low angles** - Power/energy (performance shots)
2. **High angles** - Vulnerability/scale (aerial establishing)
3. **Dutch tilts** - Tension/playfulness (pop-punk energy)
4. **Close-ups** - Emotional intensity (money shot)
5. **Wide shots** - Context/world-building
6. **Extreme close-ups** - Details (teeth sparkle, bubbles)

### Camera Movements:
- **Push in/Pull out** (dolly)
- **Truck left/right** (tracking)
- **Crane up/down** (vertical movement)
- **Orbit/Arc** (around subject)
- **Handheld shake** (raw energy, pop-punk)
- **Whip pan** (fast transitions)

### MTV Pop-Punk Style Specifics:
- Low angles on jumping/performing
- High cranes over crowd chaos
- Distorted rolls for raw attitude
- Jump cuts on beat
- Strobe/blade cuts for energy
