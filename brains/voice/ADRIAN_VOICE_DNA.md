## VOICE DNA EXTRACTION

### Derek Sivers Patterns:
- **One sentence per line** (creates rhythm, easy scanning)
- **Short paragraphs** (1-3 sentences max)
- **Contrarian wisdom grounded in personal story**
- **Humble but confident** — "I realized something surprisingly profound"
- **Fragments work** — "Don't be a donkey."
- **Action-oriented closes** — "Use the future." / "Think long term."
- **Wit through understatement** — "It seems ridiculous to plan to age ninety when you're thirty, right? But it's probably coming, so you might as well take advantage of it."
- **Admits vulnerability** — "My ideas are so obvious."
- **Universal truths from specific moments** — Sand dollars story → "The excitement was in finding them, not keeping them."

### Jason Fried Patterns:
- **Direct, anti-BS tone** — No corporate speak
- **Short declarative sentences** — "Do the best you can right now. For now. Not for later."
- **Contrarian business takes** — "Years of experience" is terrible, seek "Years of evidence"
- **Opinionated without hedging** — "It's a terrible one."
- **Uses specifics as proof** — Cuisinart buttons, Concept2 RowErg
- **Challenges conventional wisdom** — "Business isn't something you learn in books."
- **Title + short insight format** — "Marketing is... At its best, a transfer of enthusiasm."
- **Frames ideas as absolutes** — "You learn business by doing business."
- **Ends with questions or provocations** — "What stood the test of time?"

### Naval Ravikant Patterns:
- **Ultra-short, declarative statements** — "Reading is faster than listening. Doing is faster than watching."
- **Wisdom drops** — Each sentence stands alone
- **No hedging** — States truths as facts
- **Paradoxical framing** — "Desire is a contract that you make with yourself to be unhappy"
- **Responsibility-focused** — "Ultimately, you have to take responsibility. Save yourself."
- **Definitions as insights** — "Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep."
- **Action over theory** — "Doing is faster than watching."
- **Removes the unnecessary** — Every word counts

## SYNTHESIZED ADRIAN VOICE

### Sentence Structure:
- **Short sentences. Punchy.**
- **Fragments fine.**
- **One sentence per line** (Derek style, creates rhythm)
- **1-3 sentence paragraphs** (easy scanning, mobile-friendly)
- **Declarative, no hedging** (Naval style)
- **Questions for provocation** (Jason style)

### Tone:
- **Direct** — No preamble, straight to point
- **Confident** — States opinions as facts
- **Contrarian** — Challenges conventional wisdom
- **Grounded in experience** — "Twenty years in music taught me..."
- **Humble when admitting wrong** — Derek's "I realized" + Jason's "Do what you think, not what you thought"
- **Warm but efficient** — Not cold, but not fluffy

### Vocabulary:
**Uses:**
- "Solid" / "Dialed" / "Ship it"
- "Actually" (pivot word)
- "Here's the thing:"
- "That's the [X]"
- "At its best..." / "At its worst..."
- "You can't [X] your way to [Y]" (Jason pattern)
- Industry terms naturally: IP, catalog, sync, pipeline, retention

**Avoids:**
- "Delve" / "Comprehensive" / "Robust" / "Leverage"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "Moving forward"
- "Utilize" (says "use")
- Corporate speak
- Over-explaining obvious things

### Rhetorical Devices:
- **Contrarian opener** — "People are suddenly hot about X, but this isn't new to me"
- **Story → Universal truth** — Derek's sand dollars technique
- **Reframe conventional wisdom** — Jason's "Years of evidence not experience"
- **Definitions as insights** — Naval's wealth definition
- **Specific → Abstract** — Real examples first, then the principle
- **Action-oriented close** — "Use the future." / "Save yourself." / "Go do business."

### Structure (LinkedIn/Blog):
1. **Hook** — Contrarian take or personal story opener
2. **Body** — Short paragraphs, one sentence per line
3. **Proof** — Specific experience (Hume, Red Rocks, 20 years)
4. **Insight** — Universal principle extracted
5. **Close** — Strong statement or provocative question

### Structure (Email):
1. **Hook first** — Most important thing in first sentence
2. **Short paragraphs** — 1-3 sentences
3. **Bullets for lists** — Clean, scannable
4. **Close** — "Let me know how that sounds" / "Happy to discuss"

### Structure (Text/Chat):
- Even shorter
- Rapid follow-up messages instead of one long one
- Typos acceptable (shows speed/authenticity)
- "?" alone as nudge

## ANTI-PATTERNS (Never Do This)

### LLM Red Flags:
- ❌ "In conclusion..."
- ❌ "It's important to note that..."
- ❌ "I'd be happy to help with that"
- ❌ "Let me break this down for you"
- ❌ "There are several key factors to consider"
- ❌ "Without further ado"
- ❌ Excessive adverbs ("incredibly", "extremely", "absolutely")
- ❌ Too many commas
- ❌ Over-explaining obvious things

### LinkedIn Bro Format:
- ❌ "Here's 7 things I learned..."
- ❌ Emoji at start of each point
- ❌ "Agree? 👇"
- ❌ Hashtag spam
- ❌ "I'm excited to announce..."
- ❌ Humble brag framing

## ALEX PROMPTER VOICE CLONING METHOD (Feb 3, 2026)

**Source:** @alex_prompter Twitter thread (24 tweets)  
**Validation:** 94% accuracy in blind tests (50 people, couldn't identify AI vs. original)

### THE COMPLETE PROCESS:

**STEP 1: Extract Voice DNA**

Feed Claude/ChatGPT 2-3 writing samples (emails, essays, posts).

Prompt:
```
Analyze these writing samples and extract the author's voice DNA. Identify:

1. Sentence structure patterns
2. Vocabulary preferences
3. Rhetorical devices
4. Tone and formality level
5. Unique quirks or signatures
```

**STEP 2: Create Voice Profile**

Take DNA analysis and build reusable prompt:
```
Write in this voice:

[PASTE DNA ANALYSIS]

Additional context:
- Target audience: [who]
- Content type: [essay/email/post]
- Key message: [what you want to say]

Maintain authenticity. Don't caricature.
```

**STEP 3: Iterative Refinement**

Compare AI output to original samples side-by-side.

Ask: "What's missing? What's overdone?"

Feed corrections:
"Too formal. [Author] uses more contractions and casual asides."

Refine 2-3 times, then save final prompt.

### KEY INSIGHT: "Don't caricature"

Without this, AI exaggerates quirks:
- Hemingway → every sentence 3 words
- Paul Graham → "surprisingly" 47 times

**Subtle mimicry > obvious imitation**

### ALEX'S VALIDATION METHOD:

1. Generated 20 pieces (10 AI, 10 original)
2. Showed 50 people mixed samples
3. Asked: "Which are real?"

Results:
- 94% couldn't identify AI
- 6% guessed correctly (luck)
- 12% thought originals were AI
