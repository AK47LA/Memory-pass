## HIT ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

**What makes a Silly song a HIT:**

1. **SENSORY DETAIL** (taste, touch, sound, sight, emotion)
   - Good: "Yummy paste upon my tongue"
   - Bad: "Minty paste" (not all paste is mint)

2. **CHARACTER/PERSONALITY** (things have character)
   - Good: "Bubbles dancing, having fun"
   - Bad: "Look at the bubbles" (functional)

3. **CONCRETE IMAGERY** (specific, visual)
   - Good: "Tiny circles, nice and slow"
   - Bad: "Brush in circles" (generic)

4. **EMOTIONAL VALIDATION** (acknowledge struggle first)
   - Good: "Looks kinda weird, smells okay"
   - Bad: Just telling what to do

5. **ONE-WORD HOOKS** (extreme repetition like Max Martin)
   - Good: "TASTE it, TASTE it, TASTE it NOW!"
   - Good: "Brush it UP! ... Brush it DOWN!"
   - Bad: "Just TRY it, then you'll KNOW" (two ideas)

6. **KID VOCABULARY ONLY** (4-year-old words)
   - Good: "yummy", "bubbles", "tiny"
   - Bad: "pearly whites", "magnificent", "commence"

7. **WE/OUR PERSPECTIVE** (not YOU - not lecturing)
   - Good: "WE brush OUR teeth"
   - Bad: "YOU brush YOUR teeth"
